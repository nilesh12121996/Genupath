Core features — what makes someone pay for this
01
Application probability score — "Should I even apply?"
Before a candidate applies, they see a real-time probability estimate: 34% chance of progressing to phone screen. Calculated from: JD keyword match against their profile, number of current applicants, company's historical hiring patterns, H1B sponsorship likelihood, seniority gap, and location match. Like Zillow's Zestimate — not a guarantee, but an honest signal that changes behavior.
Candidate-facing ML model Gets better with data
02
Anonymised competitor view — "Who am I up against?"
Candidates see an aggregated, fully anonymised view of other applicants for the same role: distribution of years of experience, top skills in the pool, highest-represented previous companies, education levels. No individual profiles — only statistical distributions. Think of it like seeing the histogram of bids on a house, not who bid. This is the feature no one else has and everyone wants.
Candidate-facing Privacy-safe Needs volume to be useful
03
Honest resume optimisation — truthful, not fabricated
Claude Sonnet rewrites your resume bullets to match JD language — but is constrained to your actual experience (the truthfulness gate). The key difference from the stealth tool: the candidate reviews every change before it goes out. It's an AI writing assistant, not an auto-submitter. This is fully legal, fully ethical, and something Word and Google Docs cannot do.
Candidate-facing Fully legal Claude Sonnet
04
Candidate quality score — for companies
Every applicant who applies through CareerPilot arrives pre-scored on a 0–100 scale. HR teams see a ranked list of applicants, not a pile of PDFs. Score is computed from: JD keyword match, years of relevant experience, skills overlap, gap analysis, and historical signal (did similar profiles get hired elsewhere on the platform?). Companies don't buy resume parsing — they buy ranked, pre-screened candidates.
Company-facing ATS integration Saves 60% sourcing time
05
H1B sponsorship intelligence — real data, not guesses
The platform maintains a live database of employer LCA filings from DOL OFLC, enriched with crowd-sourced sponsorship outcomes from CareerPilot users (anonymised). A candidate sees: "Google sponsored 847 H1B visas last year. 94% of CareerPilot users who reached final round at Google received an offer with sponsorship." This is the most valuable signal for the F1/OPT community and does not exist anywhere else in this form.
Visa candidates DOL OFLC data Crowd-sourced enrichment
06
Interview prep + offer prediction — after the application
When an interview is booked, the system auto-generates JD-specific STAR questions, technical prep, and a company brief. After the interview, candidates log how it went — this feeds the outcome prediction model. Over time CareerPilot can predict: "Based on your interview description, you have a 61% chance of receiving an offer." No other platform has post-application intelligence.

Legal architecture — how every feature is defensible

The original design's scraping approach would have resulted in cease-and-desist letters from LinkedIn, Indeed, and major ATS providers within weeks. The new architecture eliminates every legal risk by inverting the relationship: platforms give us data because they want to, not because we take it.

Core legal principle

Every data flow is either (a) official API with terms agreed to, (b) user-consented data about themselves, or (c) publicly available government datasets. No scraping of user profiles. No fake browser sessions. No ToS violations.

Why companies will embrace this

Companies are drowning in irrelevant applications. A platform that pre-screens candidates and sends ranked, scored applicants directly to their ATS is a product they will pay for. We become a sourcing channel, not an adversary.

Every data source, legally justified
Job postings from companies
Companies post directly via our API or integrate their ATS (Greenhouse, Lever, Workday webhooks). They consent to their jobs being listed. Zero scraping.
Candidate profile data
Candidates upload their own resume and enter their own data. They explicitly consent to it being used for matching and anonymised competitor analytics. GDPR-compliant with right-to-delete.
H1B sponsorship data
DOL OFLC H1B Disclosure Data is a US government public dataset — free to use commercially. We enrich it with consented outcome data from users who opt in to share results.
Competitor view analytics
Only statistical distributions of anonymised data — no individual profiles exposed. Similar to how Glassdoor shows salary distributions. GDPR Article 89 research exemption applies where needed.
Company data (Crunchbase, Glassdoor)
We use official APIs with agreed terms. Glassdoor API is available to approved partners. Crunchbase has a commercial API. No scraping — API relationships are part of the business BD roadmap.
What we explicitly do NOT do (and why that's a moat)
No fake browser sessions

Every application is submitted by the actual candidate via their own authenticated session, or via official ATS API integration. No Playwright. No proxy rotation. No impersonation.

No automated submissions without review

The platform prepares the application — resume tailored, cover letter drafted — but the candidate clicks Submit. They see what's going out. This is the critical legal and ethical boundary.

No scraping competitor profiles

We never scrape LinkedIn or any platform for other people's data. The competitor view is built exclusively from our own users who consent to their anonymised data being included in aggregate statistics.

The moat — what makes this hard to copy
Data network effect

The probability model and competitor view only get accurate with volume. The first 10,000 users create a dataset that makes the product dramatically better than it was at 1,000. This is a classic data moat — incumbents can't copy it without the historical data.

Outcome data monopoly

Every time a CareerPilot user gets an offer, rejects an offer, or gets rejected, that outcome is (with consent) fed back into the model. After 2 years of operation, the offer prediction accuracy is effectively impossible to replicate without re-running 2 years of history.

H1B community ownership

The F1/OPT/H4 community is underserved, tightly networked, and has extremely high willingness to pay for accurate sponsorship intelligence. Once CareerPilot becomes the go-to for this community, word-of-mouth CAC drops to near-zero.
